Benedict Mitchell is an artist based in Parramatta in Western Sydney.

Benedict's practice is primarily concerned with the representation of everyday objects and behaviours in the Australian context.

Benedict creates sculpture and wall hangings primarily using papier mache, clay, and pastels; pieces which he encourages to be enjoyed within the home.

His experience growing up in the Blue Mountains has fostered an appreciation and respect for natural resources in his work.

Benedict wishes he still had his 1999 model mazda 323 which "ran like a champ" and one day hopes for his older brother to let him have a turn on the playstation.

When not creating art, Benedict spends his time collecting photos of gerry harvey from old newspapers and reading the minutes of past annual general meetings from the revesby workers club.

Multiple sources can verify that Benedict has never seen a toyota tarago in person and cannot taste the difference between sand and dirt.
